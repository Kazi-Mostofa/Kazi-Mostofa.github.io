Probabilistic Load Forecasting using CatBoost Quantile Regression

Machine Learning Time Series Energy Forecasting

Introduction & Problem Statement

Accurate load forecasting is critical for efficient energy management, grid stability, and economic dispatch planning. Traditional deterministic forecasting methods fail to capture uncertainty in energy consumption patterns, leading to suboptimal scheduling decisions and increased operational costs. This project addresses the need for probabilistic forecasts that provide confidence intervals and uncertainty quantification for building-level thermal and electrical loads.

Methodology

Data Preprocessing & Feature Engineering:

  • Analyzed 24-hour and 15-minute resolution building energy consumption data
  • Implemented seasonal encoding using sine and cosine transformations for cyclical temporal patterns
  • Integrated meteorological data (temperature, humidity, solar radiation) as exogenous variables
  • Created holiday and special event indicators to capture anomalous consumption patterns
  • Applied data normalization and handled missing values using advanced imputation techniques

Model Development:

  • Implemented CatBoost gradient boosting algorithm with quantile regression objectives
  • Configured multiple quantile targets (10th, 25th, 50th, 75th, 90th percentiles) for comprehensive uncertainty estimation
  • Performed hyperparameter tuning using grid search and cross-validation
  • Optimized learning rate, tree depth, and iterations for model performance

Results & Outcomes

  • Forecasting Accuracy: Achieved superior prediction intervals with 90% coverage while maintaining narrow uncertainty bounds
  • Performance Metrics: Significantly improved MAPE and RMSE compared to baseline Random Forest and MVLR models
  • Seasonal Adaptation: Model successfully captured winter heating peaks and summer cooling loads with high precision
  • Computational Efficiency: Training time reduced by 40% compared to ensemble methods while maintaining accuracy

Impact & Applications

This probabilistic forecasting framework enables:

  • Risk-aware energy procurement and scheduling decisions for building operators
  • Enhanced microgrid dispatch optimization through uncertainty-aware planning
  • Improved battery storage scheduling with confidence-based charge/discharge strategies
  • Cost savings through reduced over-provisioning and demand charge management

CHP Microgrid Dispatch Optimization with MILP

Optimization MILP Energy Management

Introduction & Problem Statement

Microgrids integrating Combined Heat and Power (CHP) systems with Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) require sophisticated optimization to balance electrical and thermal demands while minimizing operational costs. The challenge lies in coordinating multiple energy sources, managing storage charge/discharge cycles, and optimizing grid interactions under time-varying electricity prices and demand profiles.

Methodology

System Modeling:

  • Developed comprehensive mathematical model for CHP unit with electrical and thermal output constraints
  • Modeled BESS charge/discharge dynamics with state-of-charge limits and efficiency losses
  • Incorporated grid import/export capabilities with time-of-use pricing structures
  • Implemented thermal storage dynamics and building heat demand profiles

Optimization Framework:

  • Formulated Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) problem for 24-hour dispatch optimization
  • Objective function: Minimize total operational cost including fuel, grid electricity, and maintenance
  • Constraints: Power balance, thermal balance, equipment capacity limits, ramp rates, minimum up/down times
  • Binary variables for CHP on/off states and commitment decisions
  • Implemented penalty stack variables for efficient thermal energy management and demand response

Solution Approach:

  • Utilized Gurobi optimizer for MILP solving with optimality gap tolerance of 1%
  • Implemented rolling horizon optimization for real-time dispatch updates
  • Integrated short-term load forecasting for enhanced scheduling accuracy

Results & Outcomes

  • Cost Reduction: Achieved 22% reduction in operational costs compared to baseline rule-based control
  • Efficiency Improvement: Increased overall system efficiency by optimizing CHP utilization during peak thermal demand periods
  • Grid Interaction: Reduced peak grid imports by 35% through strategic battery dispatch and CHP scheduling
  • Computational Performance: Solved 24-hour optimization problems in under 30 seconds, enabling real-time implementation
  • Renewable Integration: Demonstrated capability to integrate with solar PV forecasts for enhanced sustainability

Impact & Applications

This optimization framework provides:

  • Scalable solution for campus microgrids, industrial facilities, and remote communities
  • Foundation for DOE-GE Vernova collaboration on Tok, Alaska microgrid project
  • Enhanced energy resilience through optimal resource coordination during grid outages
  • Reduced carbon emissions through efficient CHP utilization and renewable integration
  • Economic benefits from reduced electricity bills and demand charge management
