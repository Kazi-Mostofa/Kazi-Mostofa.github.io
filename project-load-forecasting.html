Probabilistic Load Forecasting using CatBoost Quantile Regression

Machine Learning Time Series Analysis Energy Forecasting Python

Abstract

This research project develops a sophisticated probabilistic load forecasting system using CatBoost gradient boosting with quantile regression. The system provides uncertainty-aware predictions for building-level thermal and electrical loads, enabling risk-informed decision-making for energy management, economic dispatch, and grid optimization. By generating prediction intervals across multiple quantiles (10th, 25th, 50th, 75th, 90th percentiles), the model quantifies forecast uncertainty and significantly outperforms traditional deterministic approaches while maintaining computational efficiency for real-time applications.

üéØ Problem Statement & Motivation

Accurate load forecasting is fundamental to efficient energy management, grid stability, and economic dispatch planning in modern power systems. However, traditional deterministic forecasting methods provide only point estimates without quantifying prediction uncertainty, leading to several critical challenges:

  • Suboptimal Scheduling Decisions: Without uncertainty bounds, energy system operators cannot assess risk levels or make informed decisions about reserve margins and contingency planning
  • Increased Operational Costs: Over-conservative planning to compensate for uncertainty leads to excessive energy procurement and unnecessary reserve capacity
  • Grid Instability: Unexpected load variations without probabilistic bounds can cause frequency deviations and voltage instabilities
  • Inefficient Resource Allocation: Inability to quantify confidence levels prevents optimal allocation of generation resources and battery storage
  • Poor Microgrid Coordination: Deterministic forecasts fail to capture demand uncertainty, hindering effective coordination of distributed energy resources

This project addresses these challenges by developing a probabilistic forecasting framework that provides comprehensive uncertainty quantification while maintaining the accuracy and computational efficiency required for practical deployment in building energy management systems and microgrid dispatch optimization.

üî¨ Methodology & Technical Approach

1. Data Acquisition & Preprocessing

The project utilized high-resolution building energy consumption data with the following characteristics:

  • Temporal Resolution: Dual resolution datasets (24-hour aggregated and 15-minute interval data) spanning multiple years
  • Load Types: Both thermal loads (heating/cooling) and electrical loads from commercial and educational buildings
  • Meteorological Integration: External weather data including ambient temperature, humidity, solar radiation, wind speed, and cloud cover
  • Calendar Features: Day of week, month, season, holiday indicators, and special event flags

Data Quality Assurance

  • Outlier detection and treatment using statistical methods (IQR and Z-score)
  • Missing value imputation using forward-fill, interpolation, and k-NN techniques
  • Data validation against physical constraints (e.g., non-negative loads, realistic ranges)
  • Removal of meter malfunction periods and calibration errors

2. Feature Engineering

Advanced feature engineering techniques were implemented to capture complex temporal and seasonal patterns:

  • Cyclical Encoding: Sine and cosine transformations for hour-of-day, day-of-week, and month-of-year to preserve cyclical nature
  • Lag Features: Historical load values at strategic intervals (1-hour, 24-hour, 168-hour lags) to capture autoregressive patterns
  • Rolling Statistics: Moving averages, standard deviations, and quantiles over multiple window sizes (24h, 7d, 30d)
  • Weather Interaction Terms: Combined features like temperature-humidity index, heating/cooling degree days, and solar-temperature interactions
  • Calendar Interactions: Workday/weekend distinctions, holiday proximity, semester periods for educational buildings
  • Rate-of-Change Features: Load gradients and acceleration metrics to capture demand dynamics

3. Model Architecture & Training

CatBoost Gradient Boosting with Quantile Regression:

  • Algorithm Selection: CatBoost chosen for superior handling of categorical features, built-in regularization, and computational efficiency
  • Quantile Targets: Simultaneous training of five quantile models (œÑ = 0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90) to generate comprehensive prediction intervals
  • Loss Function: Pinball loss (quantile loss) optimized for each target quantile independently
  • Hyperparameter Optimization: Grid search with 5-fold cross-validation over learning rate (0.01-0.1), tree depth (4-10), iterations (500-2000), L2 regularization (1-10)
  • Training Strategy: Walk-forward validation to simulate real-time deployment, with expanding training window and fixed test horizon
  • Early Stopping: Monitoring validation performance to prevent overfitting while maximizing model capacity

Model Configuration

Optimal Hyperparameters (after tuning):

  • Learning Rate: 0.05
  • Tree Depth: 7
  • Iterations: 1200
  • L2 Leaf Regularization: 3.0
  • Bootstrap Type: Bernoulli (probability = 0.8)
  • Random Strength: 1.5

4. Model Validation & Performance Evaluation

Comprehensive evaluation metrics were employed to assess both point forecast accuracy and probabilistic forecast quality:

Point Forecast Metrics (50th percentile):

  • Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE)
  • Root Mean Square Error (RMSE)
  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE)
  • Coefficient of Variation (CV-RMSE)

Probabilistic Forecast Metrics:

  • Prediction Interval Coverage Probability (PICP): Measures if actual values fall within predicted intervals at target confidence levels
  • Prediction Interval Normalized Average Width (PINAW): Assesses interval sharpness while maintaining coverage
  • Winkler Score: Combined metric penalizing both wide intervals and poor coverage
  • Continuous Ranked Probability Score (CRPS): Evaluates entire predictive distribution quality

Key Findings & Performance Highlights

  • Superior Point Forecast Accuracy: CatBoost quantile regression achieved MAPE of 4.2% for day-ahead forecasts, significantly outperforming Random Forest (5.8% MAPE) and MVLR (7.3% MAPE) baseline models
  • Excellent Probabilistic Calibration: 90% prediction intervals achieved 90.2% coverage probability, demonstrating well-calibrated uncertainty estimates
  • Narrow Uncertainty Bounds: Despite high coverage, prediction interval width remained 15% narrower than Random Forest quantile regression, providing actionable uncertainty estimates
  • Seasonal Robustness: Model successfully captured extreme winter heating peaks (within 3% error) and summer cooling loads (within 4% error) with appropriate uncertainty scaling
  • Computational Efficiency: Training time reduced by 40% compared to Random Forest ensemble, with inference time under 50ms per prediction‚Äîsuitable for real-time applications
  • Feature Importance Insights: Temperature (28%), hour-of-day (22%), and lag features (25%) dominated predictions, with day-type and calendar features contributing 15% collectively
  • Extreme Event Handling: Model showed enhanced performance during unusual conditions (holidays, extreme weather) through well-calibrated wider prediction intervals

Benchmark Comparison

Performance vs State-of-the-Art Methods:

  • CatBoost Quantile: 4.2% MAPE, 90.2% PICP, 0.18 PINAW
  • Random Forest Quantile: 5.8% MAPE, 88.5% PICP, 0.21 PINAW
  • MVLR: 7.3% MAPE (deterministic only)
  • LSTM Neural Network: 5.1% MAPE, 91.5% PICP, 0.25 PINAW (3x longer training)

Seasonal & Temporal Performance Analysis

  • Winter Performance: Heating load predictions maintained 4.5% MAPE during peak demand periods with appropriately wider prediction intervals during cold snaps
  • Summer Performance: Cooling load forecasts achieved 4.0% MAPE with excellent capture of air conditioning cycling patterns
  • Shoulder Seasons: Lowest MAPE of 3.8% during spring and fall due to reduced load volatility and moderate weather conditions
  • Intraday Patterns: Morning ramp-up and evening peak periods captured with 5-6% MAPE, slightly higher than mid-day stable periods (3-4% MAPE)
  • Weekend vs Weekday: Distinct patterns successfully modeled with weekend MAPE of 4.8% vs weekday 4.0%, reflecting different occupancy and usage patterns

‚úÖ Conclusions

This project successfully developed and validated a state-of-the-art probabilistic load forecasting system using CatBoost quantile regression. The system achieves superior performance across multiple evaluation criteria:

  • Accuracy: 4.2% MAPE significantly outperforms baseline deterministic and probabilistic methods
  • Calibration: Prediction intervals exhibit excellent coverage probability matching theoretical targets
  • Efficiency: Computational performance enables real-time deployment in operational energy management systems
  • Robustness: Consistent performance across seasons, day-types, and extreme weather events
  • Actionability: Narrow yet well-calibrated prediction intervals provide practical uncertainty quantification

The demonstrated capabilities position this probabilistic forecasting framework as a valuable tool for building energy management, microgrid optimization, and grid operations. Quantified forecast uncertainty enables risk-aware decision-making, leading to operational cost savings, improved reliability, and enhanced sustainability outcomes.
